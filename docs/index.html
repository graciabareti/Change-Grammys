Overview

The Grammys have been around since 1958, yet you’d think that a platform of such stature would have more than only 17% of nominees being female in 20182. Or there only being 10 Black artists that have won album of the year as lead artists as our reality 3. Music stems from artists pouring a piece of themselves within their craft, so why do we continue to not acknowledge Black and female artists? To further understand the biased trends within the music industry, we used data collected from research at California Polytechnic State University that focused on Grammy and Billboard results from 1999-2019 4.

5

Gender Trends

#Join Billboard and Grammy data set in order to only have songs that 
#were applicable for both. This was followed by filtering out by max 
#week in order to get most accurate amounts recent streams.

billboard_new<-billboard%>%
group_by(Name)%>%
filter(Week == max(Week))
join_grammy_billboard<-song_results%>%
left_join(billboard_new, by="Name")%>%
filter(!is.na(GrammyAward))

#Joining songs that were both Grammy Award winning and featured on
#the Billboards with data set that included the gender of solo artists.
gender_GB<-join_grammy_billboard%>%
left_join(Gender, by="Artist")%>%
filter(!is.na(Gender))%>%
group_by(Gender, GrammyYear)%>%
summarize(count=n())

ggplot(data =gender_GB, mapping = aes(x = GrammyYear, y = count, 
color = Gender))+
geom_line()+
ggtitle(' ')+
labs(x="Grammy Year",y="Grammy Award")+
scale_color_manual(values=c("purple","red"))+
ggtitle("Grammy Winners By Gender",
  subtitle="From 1999-2019")+
theme(plot.title = element_text(face = "bold"))

The visualization above demonstrates the total number of Grammy Award-winning while also Billboard charting solo artists by male and female. We filtered out the Grammy winners by their Billboard performance as well so we could get a better representation of the song’s performance given the history of bias within the Grammys. From our work, we noticed a trend of men significantly being awarded at a greater amount from 1999-2008. Though from 2008-2015, the male and female numbers seemed to be closer but didn’t last as after 2015, males had received a significantly greater amount than females.
